How It Began

A humble loaf, a big dream

In 2014, husband-and-wife team Mei Lin and Ethan Chow converted a ground-floor shophouse at 56 Eng Hoon Street into a bakery that Tiong Bahru had long deserved. Ethan trained under sourdough masters in Lyon; Mei Lin spent a decade in Singapore's specialty-coffee scene. Together they built a place where bread is treated with the same reverence as fine wine — shaped by hand, leavened by live cultures, and baked on a hearth stone at 260 °C.

  • Naturally leavened with our own 10-year-old starter, 'Eng Hoon'
  • Heritage flours stone-milled fresh, imported from France and Australia
  • Zero artificial preservatives — what you see is what we bake
  • Open kitchen every morning from 6 am so you can watch the craft

What We Stand For

Our four guiding principles

🌾

Honest Ingredients

We name every ingredient on our menu. No mystery additives, no shortcuts — just flour, water, salt and time.

♻️

Mindful Waste

Day-old bread becomes croutons, puddings and porridge. Spent coffee grounds go to urban garden partners. We target zero food waste by 2027.

🤝

Community First

Every Saturday morning we run free bread-making workshops for Tiong Bahru residents. We are proud to be a true neighbourhood bakery.

☕

Slow Craft

Speed is the enemy of good bread. We ferment for 48–72 hours, laminate pastry over two days, and hand-pull every espresso shot.

🌍

Responsible Sourcing

Our coffee is direct-trade from farms in Ethiopia, Colombia and Sumatra. Our dairy is hormone-free. We choose suppliers who share our ethics.

🎓

Lifelong Learning

Our bakers attend international craft workshops yearly. We host visiting artisans from Japan, France and Scandinavia for pop-up collaborations.
